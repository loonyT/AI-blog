How to avoid common mistakes when measuring your service desk performance.

Checkout the most common mistakes

Posted by Bastien Deresteau on the 7th of March 2021

When you want to optimize your service desk, you need precise metrics and reports. Yet, some data are more relevant than others. So, when you select the measures, you have to pay attention to common mistakes.

Here are six of them:

  1. Wrong data selected

    2. Each metric must have a specific purpose. So, when you choose one to analyze, ask yourself the following question:

    Which problem needs to be solved and what is the objective?

  2. Selecting too many metrics

    3. Current ITSM tools provide a lot of reports and measures. Thus, your agents will spend lots of time in the filtering process. This costs money and that is why you should only select the relevant measures

  3. Irrelevant metrics

    4. For a metric to be worthy of interest. It has to be in line with the companyâ€™s expectations. Otherwise, it is useless

  4. Context mistakes

    5. You must not select a metric out of the blue. You have to take the context into account.

  5. Blindly follow the competition and industry references

    6. Each organization has its specificities. Hence, a relevant metric for one company could be useless for another.

  6. Forget to upgrade your statistics

    7. For a business to remain competitive, it has to transform and adapt daily. Consequently, your teams and metrics must stay up to date.

Relevant metrics are importants!.

If you are a Service Desk manager, you want to have the proper metrics as quickly as possible. This is not an easy process and there are lots of potential mistakes you can encounter.

  • The average duration of a conversation
  • The average time spent by end-users/customers in queues
  • Customer concerns and location
  • Team workload and efficiency
