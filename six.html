How to optimize the cost of your tickets within your Service Desk?

Posted by Bastien Deresteau on the seventh of March 2021

To get the cost of a ticket, you can use this formula:

(Total running cost of your service center) / (number of tickets resolved during the same period)

For relevant metrics, you can compare that cost with the satisfaction rate of an end-user (SRE) and the first call resolution rate (FCRR).

If the cost of a ticket and the SRE are high, you know that you do not need more training or more agents for your contact center. Thus, you have to contain your costs elsewhere (in the software or with hidden costs for instance).

On the other hand, if the cost of a ticket is high but SRE and FCRR are low, there is a problem. In that case, you can hire more agents or improve your training processes, to reduce the overall cost.

Ticket's costs can hamper your business productivity

Here are six factors that can have an impact on the cost of your tickets:

  1. Size of your team

    2. One of the most important factors: If the size is too low, you have a risk of burnout for your agents and more overtime hours. If the size is too big, your costs are raising without added value.

  2. Cost per hour per agent

    3. If you have high hourly costs, your cost per ticket will rise. The wages of your agents can have a high impact on your exploitation costs. Thus, you can optimize them to lower the price of a ticket.

  3. Agent's flow

    4. Truancy and a high turnover within your workforce will impact your ticket costs. Such as the extra hours and the training of the new employees.

  4. Agent's productivity

    5. The more your agents can deliver, the lower the cost of your ticket will be.

  5. Infrastructure costs

    6. You have to optimize it. To do so, you can analyze what is the most profitable:

    Hire physical agents that handle your contact center in a building. Or, outsource the requests to remote workers.
