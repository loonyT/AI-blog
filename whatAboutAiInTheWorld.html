How to reduce the cost of your communication bills using a Chatbot?

First, what is a Chatbot? It is a conversational agent, powered by AI that speaks with a user to process her/his requests. Hence, it appears logical to use it in your ITSM

Posted by Bastien Deresteau on the 7th of March 2021

Second, is it necessary and cost-friendly? To answer, a slice of history can be relevant. From the call center to the hotline, from the help desk to the service desk, the idea of phone support in IT has always existed. And the only thing that has not changed in fifty years is the change itself!

This means that the idea constantly adapts and evolves. Also, support has spread progressively. Now, it encompasses all IT means a company can have. Thus, the Service Desk is now more a Business Service Desk that has to answer all IT requests coming from the company. It is not limited anymore to the information system.

Consequently, the cost of daily communications have raised. Likewise, the workload of IT supports increasingly raise. Due to that, it can be smart to use a Chatbot to reduce these raises, such as Swisscom, for its customer service.

Furthermore, the ICT journal has shown that, communication through chat, was one of the most popular channels among users of a Service Desk. Especially among millennials. Besides, self-service portals are also becoming more and more popular.

Reducing your communicatons bills

What is relevant, to reduce your phone bill, is that a « talking bot » combine the advantages of the self-service:

  • Available all the time
  • Available on every support
  • Available everywhere

With those of instant messaging:

  • Rapidity
  • User friendly
  • Convenience

Hence, by using a Chatbot for your ITSM requests, you can get rid of the long waiting queues. Consequently, you can contact a support agent faster. At least for first-line type of issues (for instance: a change of password).

Also, over time, conversational AI tools will be able to answer more complex questions. Especially in the banking sector where they are increasingly replacing text messages, emails, phone. Thus, adapting to users who are more and more tech enthusiasts. In the end, Chatbot could answer more than 80% of customer requests.
